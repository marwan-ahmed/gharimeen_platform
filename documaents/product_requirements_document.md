## 1. Executive Summary

*   **Goal:** Provide a high-level overview of the project, its main purpose, and expected outcomes. This should be concise and easily digestible for busy stakeholders.
*   **Context:** [Briefly describe the current situation or problem that this project aims to address. E.g., "Our current customer support system is overwhelmed, leading to long wait times and low customer satisfaction."]
*   **Proposed Solution:** [Briefly describe what the project will deliver. E.g., "This project will develop a new self-service portal, empowering customers to resolve common issues independently."]
*   **Expected Impact:** [Briefly state the anticipated benefits. E.g., "We expect to reduce support tickets by 30% and improve customer satisfaction scores by 15% within six months of launch."]

---

## 2. Problem/Opportunity Statement

*   **Goal:** Clearly articulate the problem you are solving or the opportunity you are seizing. This section should be customer-centric and data-driven if possible.
*   **The Problem:** [Describe the pain point, gap, or challenge in detail. Who is affected? What are the current consequences? Use data or anecdotal evidence if available. E.g., "Our customers frequently call support for simple tasks like password resets or checking order status. This costs us $X per call and frustrates customers who prefer digital solutions."]
*   **The Opportunity:** [Describe the potential market, efficiency gain, revenue increase, or strategic advantage this project unlocks. E.g., "By offering a robust self-service platform, we can reduce operational costs, free up support agents for complex issues, and enhance our brand reputation for convenience and innovation."]

---

## 3. Vision & Goals

*   **Goal:** Define the long-term aspiration and specific, measurable objectives for this project.

### 3.1. Product Vision (for product-focused PRDs)

*   **Goal:** A concise, inspiring statement of the ultimate desired future state the product aims to achieve. This is a guiding star.
*   **Vision Statement:** [E.g., "To empower our customers with effortless, immediate access to solutions and information, making their experience with our brand seamless and satisfying."]

### 3.2. Project Goals & Objectives (SMART)

*   **Goal:** Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound objectives that define what success looks like for *this specific project*.
*   **Objective 1:** [E.g., "Reduce average customer support call volume by 25% within 3 months post-launch."]
    *   **Metric:** Number of support calls, self-service usage rate.
*   **Objective 2:** [E.g., "Improve customer satisfaction (CSAT) score for self-service users by 10% within 6 months post-launch."]
    *   **Metric:** CSAT surveys, NPS score.
*   **Objective 3:** [E.g., "Achieve 50% adoption rate of the new self-service portal by existing customers within 6 months post-launch."]
    *   **Metric:** Unique users, feature usage analytics.

*   **Feature Group 2: Knowledge Base**
    *   [Feature 2.1]: Search functionality for articles.

*   **Feature Group 3: Order Tracking**
    *   [Feature 3.1]: Display current and past order status.

### 6.2. Functional Requirements (User Stories)

*   **Goal:** Describe features from the user's perspective.
*   **Format:** As a [type of user], I want to [action], so that [benefit/goal].
*   **Example 1:** As a *customer*, I want to *reset my password*, so that I can *regain access to my account without contacting support*.
*   **Example 2:** As a *customer*, I want to *search for articles about billing issues*, so that I can *find answers quickly*.
*   **Example 3:** As an *admin*, I want to *publish new knowledge base articles*, so that *customers have up-to-date information*.

### 6.3. Non-Functional Requirements

*   **Goal:** Define the quality attributes and constraints of the system.

    *   [E.g., "The UI/UX must be intuitive and accessible (WCAG 2.1 AA compliant)."]

*   **Reliability/Availability:**
    *   [E.g., "The system must have 99.9% uptime."]

*   **Scalability:**
